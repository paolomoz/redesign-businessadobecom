# Adobe for Business — current state

## Register

**Brand** — marketing site that catalogues Adobe's enterprise product portfolio (Experience Cloud, Creative Cloud for business, Document Cloud, GenStudio, Firefly, Marketo, Workfront, Analytics, etc.) and routes prospects toward learning, demos, contact-sales, and pricing.

## Audience

Enterprise buyers and influencers across three loosely-defined personas the IA implies:

- **Marketing leaders** — looking for analytics, marketing automation, customer-data, journey orchestration.
- **Creative leaders** — looking for content production at scale, GenAI, asset management.
- **Document / business operations** — looking for Acrobat, signature, document workflows.

The home keeps these audiences in adjacent zones rather than forcing a persona pick at the top.

## Product purpose

Land enterprise-prospect traffic, convey portfolio breadth, and route to product detail / contact-sales. The home is heavily *announcement-driven* (Semrush acquisition, Summit 2026, AI at Adobe) — news and momentum signals are foreground, the product index is the foundation underneath.

## Brand personality

- **Authoritative.** The voice is confident, declarative ("Unleash creativity at scale", "Data and insights for a competitive edge").
- **Enterprise-serious.** Cooler register than consumer Adobe — fewer color flashes, more density, more product names per screen.
- **AI-forward.** Most cards on the current home reference AI explicitly; "AI at Adobe" is its own section.

## Anti-references

- Not a consumer site. Pricing, free trials, "creators" framing belong to adobe.com proper.
- Not a documentation site. Help, learn, and community sit on adjacent properties (helpx, experienceleague).

## Design principles (inferred)

1. **Portfolio breadth first, depth on click.** The home is an index, not a long-form narrative.
2. **Announcements lead.** Whatever Adobe shipped this quarter takes the marquee.
3. **Consistent product card.** Every product surface uses the same card primitive — eyebrow, title, "Explore products" link.
4. **Quiet, restrained palette.** Mostly white background, dark text, single saturated blue accent. Color is rare and deliberate.
5. **Editorial typography.** Adobe Clean does almost all the heavy lifting; type scale, not color, carries hierarchy.
